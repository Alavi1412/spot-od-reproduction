SPOT-OD: A Simulator-Bound Orbit-Determination Self-Audit
Record with Adaptive-Filter and Observability Mechanism Findings
Authors omitted for review
May 2026
Abstract
The primary contribution is a compact-simulator AUKF R-inflation diagnostic under force-model mismatch,
not independently validated for operational transfer. In a compact two-body+J2 +drag OD simulator,
the adaptive unscented Kalman filter (AUKF) inflates its measurement-noise scale (mean 3.32); median
R-only normalised-innovation-squared grows from 1.60 (extended Kalman filter, EKF) and 1.83 (unscented
Kalman filter, UKF) to 4.73 (AUKF). A drag-scale cascade separates EKF linearisation, sparse-geometry
observability, and β prior inertia: median β̂ = 1.0000 despite 9.6 km drag separation. A Dynamics-Bias
Adaptation-Risk heuristic is withdrawn after 450 realizations proved indistinguishable from its 81.3%
no-information baseline.
The learned-estimator bounded negative is secondary. Applied to the authors’ RGR-GF family and a
KalmanNet-style comparator, no evaluated construction beats the per-scenario best classical reference
on observed-step position-RMSE (selected post hoc; K = 32 and K = 96 draws are internal checks, not
external preregistration).
A self-audit record under self-authored definitions (not independently validated) makes findings
inspectable. Compact-model errors range 350–925 m while operational ILRS/SP3 analyses reach
centimetre/sub-metre accuracy—a distinct operational gap. For small-satellite teams, the AUKF diagnostic and drag-scale cascade inform compact-simulator screening gates before precise-reference validation
campaigns.

Keywords: orbit determination; satellite state estimation; sparse visibility; claim-audited evaluation;
adaptive filtering; negative results; reproducibility

a strong dynamics-aware baseline, which is the claim audit this study enforces.

Related Work and Positioning

Orbit propagation and statistical estimation. The study is grounded in the SGP4/NORAD lineage
and standard astrodynamics references [24, 74, 73, 47], with statistical OD providing the framework for
combining dynamics, measurements, and uncertainty [72]. This motivates a baseline-first stance: learned
methods must be evaluated against strong physical filters, not weak comparators.
Recursive filters and learned filtering. The Kalman filter [29], EKF [27] and the unscented transform
[28, 77] are standard recursive estimators. Adaptive Kalman methods motivate innovation- and smoother-based
noise/covariance adjustment; learned filtering, including KalmanNet, motivates hybrid designs [44, 33, 20, 59].
An independent physics-informed ML cislunar OD study characterises learned-solution convergence and
uncertainty, reinforcing that learned-OD evaluation must weigh calibration and uncertainty rather than
point accuracy alone [1]; a deep-neural nonlinearity detector driving an adaptive order-switching filter is
a complementary neural-aided-filter design [87]. Improvements over a fixed-noise UKF can reflect noiseadaptation already represented by the AUKF comparator. Classical adaptive filtering recovers noise statistics
only when the model structure is correct [44, 60, 27]; general robust Kalman work on heavy-tailed process
and measurement noise is adjacent context, not a direct OD baseline [79]. When the dominant residual is
a dynamics/force-model error, statistical OD prescribes a structural dynamic model compensation (DMC)
channel that augments the state with a low-frequency empirical-acceleration estimate, the classical structural
response to absorb unmodelled accelerations through a process-model channel rather than through noise
reweighting [72, 81, 70, 78]. The force-model-mismatch and structural-channel results reported below are
controlled diagnostics consistent with this lineage. Recent learned and adaptive OD studies (2023–2026)
refine these themes: a systematic LEO precise-OD review [65] surveys operational precise OD; physicsinformed neural OD, optical tracklet-correlation/IOD, and learned/adaptive filters for ground-based, SSA,
cislunar, deep-space, relative-navigation, and continuous-maneuver settings [63, 62, 58, 88, 85, 57, 17, 71, 82,
35, 86]; Bayesian/robust adaptive Kalman variants for nano-satellite and optical-tracking OD [9, 89]; and
operationally motivated adaptive orbit-recovery, drag-estimation, TLE/state-vector refinement, and hybrid
SGP4 error-learning [10, 42, 3, 64]. The 2025–2026 operational thread (cislunar IOD, optical-flow IOD, ISL
observability/OD, multi-scale short-arc initializers, and TLE global-optimization OD [68, 22, 69, 51, 36, 90, 41])
reinforces that observability and geometry control OD quality. Adjacent probabilistic-filtering and tasking
context—cislunar PGM IOD and recursive hybrid filtering, a coupled orbit-attitude/sensor-tasking framework,
and real-spaceborne GNSS adaptive EKF [52, 53, 54, 12]—are complementary: they report estimator
performance in their own regimes, while SPOT-OD asks what evidence composition is needed to screen
learned or adaptive improvement claims. SPOT-OD does not propose a new estimator; it places learned
residual methods, the noise-adaptive AUKF/PUKF family, the structural DMC-EKF, public replay, and
selector tasks under one auditable evidence boundary. Domain-adjacent neural orbit/state-estimation and
spacecraft-GNN work [75, 32] shows learned satellite inference is not new, without making those papers
direct baselines here. Lang and Jiang’s 2025 Sensors study of unscented-batch filtering for continuously
maneuvering Starlink satellites is adjacent OD/filtering context for maneuvering spacecraft, not a direct
comparator baseline for SPOT-OD [34]. Three very recent 2026 papers—a patch-transformer LEO orbit
predictor [37], an LSTM-Transformer short-term orbit-prediction model [83], and a physics-informed neural
network for predicting orbital parameters from TLE data [43]—show that the learned-orbit literature is
advancing rapidly. These works address propagation-accuracy and orbital-parameter forecasting rather than
recursive state-estimation self-audits under a fixed force model and sparse-visibility protocol, and none
provides a direct learned-versus-tuned-classical OD self-audit record; they motivate, rather than eliminate,
the guardrails that SPOT-OD provides.

Positioning vs operational POD/SLR practice and recent learned satellite estimators. Operational precise OD on satellite-laser-ranging targets routinely reaches few-centimetre orbit accuracy at
international analysis centres, with a full force model, a strict ITRF↔GCRF reduction ingesting the IERS
Earth-orientation series, precise tropospheric and centre-of-mass corrections, and dedicated ranging campaigns
coordinated through the ILRS [55, 26, 50, 25]. The public precise-reference probes here share inputs and
reference products with that practice but evaluate compact-dynamics estimators (two-body+J2 , optional

magnitudes are not an operational precise-OD result and are not comparable to centimetre-class ILRS analyses. Their constructive role is narrower: they reduce measurement parsing, frame-reduction, state-scoring,
and provenance risk before any later public-reference validation study. SPOT-OD does not propose a new
estimator but reports a disciplined, predeclared negative on the authors’ own residual constructions, leaving
the comparison against any faithfully reproduced published learned-OD system explicitly open.

Data Sources, Evaluation Scope, and Protocol

3.1

Archived data sources and scenario scope

The public-data layer uses archived CelesTrak and SatNOGS snapshots rather than live API state. CelesTrak
general-perturbation/TLE data provide public orbital element evidence [30]; SatNOGS network, observation,
and API metadata provide public ground-station and observation context [39, 40, 38]. The retained records
identify source URLs, fetch timing, record counts, and digests so replay scenarios remain fixed. Primary
accuracy tables use the nominal and stress synthetic splits. Public-catalog SGP4 replay is a bounded publiccatalog replay/reference slice rather than a discriminative leaderboard: only the offline robust batch WLS
reference produces a meaningful postfit solution. A controlled force-model/process-noise mismatch split
inflates the truth drag/SRP/process-noise force model while estimators keep the nominal compact model.
Bounded public precise-reference probes score real ILRS satellite-laser-ranging normal points for LAGEOS-1
and LAGEOS-2 [55, 26], parsed from public daily CRD version-2 files [25] archived by the ILRS/NASA
CDDIS data centers [50]. None of these slices is operational flight validation.
The public-data inventory and the per-scenario provenance digests are recorded in S-Table 37.

3.2

Evaluation tasks

SPOT-OD is an evaluation suite. The evaluated tasks are state estimation (acceptance-critical) and method
selection (an internal synthetic routing diagnostic; see S-Table 38). Stability labels serve exclusively as
computational guardrails for the engineering-failure audit defined below.
A trajectory is numerically divergent when its trajectory-level position RMSE is non-finite or exceeds
108 m, or its velocity RMSE exceeds 105 m/s; method-selection costs assign a 108 m penalty to divergent
candidates. These are computational guardrails, not physical stability certificates, so the results add a
separate engineering-failure audit at 100 km position RMSE or 100 m/s velocity RMSE.

3.3

State, dynamics, and observations

Each trajectory has a six-dimensional Earth-centered inertial state

with position in meters and velocity in meters per second. The generator uses a fourth-order Runge–Kutta

eccentricity 0–0.02, inclination 20–98 degrees. The acceleration model includes two-body gravity, J2 , drag

than flight-dynamics complete: it omits high-order geopotential, precession/nutation, empirical atmosphere,
maneuvers, and sensor-scheduling uncertainty beyond the specified perturbations.

elevation, range-rate); a station is visible at elevation ≥ 8 degrees. The nominal configuration uses eight
ground stations. Nominal measurement noise is range 30 m, azimuth/elevation 0.02 degrees, range-rate
0.08 m/s, outlier probability 0.01, outlier scale 6, dropout 0.02, with small station/clock bias. The reported
stress-test override increases range noise to 70 m, azimuth/elevation noise to 0.06 degrees, range-rate noise

3.4

Splits, seeds, and evaluation units

The main experiment uses seed 42. The dataset contains 160 nominal training, 32 nominal validation,
64 nominal test trajectories, 96 stress-training, 24 stress-validation, and 64 stress-test trajectories. The
curriculum uses nominal pretraining, mixed training, and stress fine-tuning; later validation includes a
SatNOGS replay split. All methods are scored on steps 11–119 (109 evaluated steps); nominal and stress
aggregates summarise 64 × 109 = 6976 evaluated trajectory-steps each. The paired statistical unit is the
trajectory-wise position RMSE over the same 109-step window, so Wilcoxon comparisons use n = 64 paired
trajectory values per comparison. All filters and learned models receive the same simulated measurements,
visibility masks, station geometry, and noisy initial state estimates (2500 m position, 6 m/s velocity standard
deviation); learned residual methods additionally receive the relevant filter priors.

4

Methods Compared

Estimator abbreviations are defined at first use, with a compact main-text guide in Table 1 and a longer glossary
at the start of the supplement. The compared families are classical recursive filters (EKF, UKF, AUKF),
predeclared adaptive or structural variants (PUKF, DMC-EKF, DSA-EKF), an offline robust batch WLS
OD reference, the bounded RGR residual learners and controls, and KalmanNet-related sanity/transposition
diagnostics.

4.1

Classical filtering baselines

The EKF [27], UKF, and AUKF estimate the same six-dimensional state from common
range/azimuth/elevation/range-rate observations (UKF unscented-transform parameters αUT =
0.2, βUT = 2, κUT = 0; AUKF adds innovation consistency R adaptation: rate 0.08, measurement scale
bounds 0.4–30, Huber 2.8, NIS soft gate 16). The unscented-transform beta parameter is denoted beta UT in
prose and βUT in equations; it is distinct from the drag-scale beta. Classical hyperparameters were selected
through a 48-trial tuning budget. We also include an offline robust batch weighted least squares OD reference:
per trajectory it fits one initial Cartesian state to the full visible arc using the same force/measurement
model and a Huber robust objective. This is intentionally non causal and postfit — a classical OD reference
that can use future measurements, not a deployment equivalent comparator. We additionally include a
process noise adaptive UKF (PUKF) as the symmetric counterpart of the AUKF: it holds R fixed at the
nominal vector and inflates the predicted process noise Q multiplicatively when a sliding window estimate of
the R only normalized innovation squared exceeds the chi-square-with-4-channels expectation, on the classical
statistical OD intuition that under a dynamics/force model bias the prior model, not the measurement
channel, carries the misspecified component [44, 27, 70]. The PUKF rule, thresholds, and decision predicate
are fixed in a timestamped rule record.
Ensemble Kalman and sequential Monte Carlo OD/data-assimilation methods are a distinct comparator
family rather than a small variant of the tuned EKF/UKF/AUKF guardrail used here; they introduce
ensemble-size, stochastic process-parameter, resampling, and sampling-noise design choices, and recent OD
work explicitly evaluates EnKF and particle-filter tracking/parameter-estimation variants in that family
[71]. To close the ensemble-Gaussian-filter exclusion rather than only cite it, we add one canonical untuned
stochastic perturbed-observation Ensemble Kalman Filter (EnKF) [15, 6] as a bounded auxiliary comparator:
a 64-member ensemble propagated through the same compact two-body, J2 , and exponential-drag dynamics
and updated with the same range/azimuth/elevation/range-rate measurement, process-noise, and azimuth
de-weighting model as the EKF/UKF/AUKF baselines, with inflation and localization deliberately left off
and a per-trajectory seed fixed in an internally frozen, hash-recorded rule that is an internal pre-evaluation
record, not an externally timestamped pre-registration. Because the EnKF shares its process-noise and
initial-covariance settings with the UKF and AUKF, the controlled comparison is EnKF versus UKF/AUKF,
while the EnKF-versus-EKF gap additionally reflects the EKF’s slightly larger tuned process noise. On the
force-mismatch, nominal, and stress slices the untuned EnKF does not displace the classical filters (S-Table 4),
7

so it is reported as a bounded result for this single untuned EnKF configuration; tuned, inflated, or localized
EnKF variants and particle/Gaussian-mixture filters remain outside this comparator set.

4.2

Learned estimator architecture and inputs

Station nodes contain the scaled four-channel measurement, a visibility indicator, Earth-radius-scaled station
coordinates, and optional innovation features. Graph-enabled models apply message-passing layers over an
implicit fully connected station graph; graph-disabled variants either skip those layers or replace them with
equal-depth local per-station layers. Visible embeddings are pooled and passed through a bidirectional GRU
over a 12-step window. The training uses hidden dim 192, five graph layers (when enabled), two GRU layers,
dropout 0.10, batch 128, lr 10−3 , weight decay 10−4 , grad-clip 1.0, mixed precision, early-stop patience 10,
and a 24/18/12-epoch nominal/mixed/stress curriculum. The full architecture and regularizer weights are in
Appendix 14; the supplementary evidence materials supplied for independent inspection provide the complete
reproduction records.
Hyperparameter and selection transparency. All compared families expose their fixed settings either

Endpoint terminology (defined once, used throughout). This study is exploratory: the commitments
are internal predeclarations supplied with the submission, with timestamped records where the record format
supports them, not external trial-registry preregistration or confirmatory validation. Predeclared means
a metric, threshold, or decision rule written down before the run that produced its result. The K = 16
frozen-rule extension has a confidential RFC3161 timestamp of its rule hash (hash disclosed, not content); the
timestamp confirms rule-before-evaluation ordering for that draw but is not public preregistration and does
not upgrade the observed-step endpoint itself from post-hoc to externally predeclared. Evidentiary control
comes from retained internal rule records, fixed seeds/splits, and digest-linked evaluation records, which make
post-hoc rule substitution inspectable under internal frozen-rule consistency but do not provide confirmatory
external validation.
Endpoint timeline. The observed-step endpoint evidence is segmented as follows:
1. Observed-step RMSE was selected on training-cohort data after a post-hoc recomputation and before the
later K = 32 replication was generated or evaluated.
2. The K = 8 endpoint-fixation support/pre-registration record inside the evidence package then froze and
checked that selected endpoint on a seed-disjoint draw; the JSON records fixed before results: true,
but it has no created/finalized timestamp field and was not externally registered or externally timestamped.

was generated or evaluated; its evidence-package record identifies an internal frozen rule and

statistical detectability. This is a mission-agnostic audit threshold for this compact simulator, not a universal
OD mission requirement; a mission-specific OD campaign would replace it with its own acceptance threshold.

Significance uses paired trajectory-wise position RMSE; the difference is baseline minus candidate (positive
favours the candidate). The one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank alternative is “candidate better” [80]. Mean-gain
intervals are percentile paired-bootstrap intervals with shared trajectory indices, at least 3000 bootstrap
resamples, and 95% confidence [14]; endpoint-specific tables state the exact count, with structural-channel
tables using 5000 paired resamples. These intervals treat the trajectory as the exchangeable resampling
unit within a declared slice; they preserve within-trajectory temporal dependence, including within-pass
residual correlation, but do not turn consecutive visible updates into independent units. The limitation is
that trajectory-pair resampling does not cover dependence from repeated trajectory identities across trained
seeds, simulator-design uncertainty, selection uncertainty, possible correlation among similarly generated
passes, or transfer beyond the finite simulator and public-probe populations. The low visible-step fraction
(roughly 20%) limits but does not eliminate within-pass correlation as an uncertainty concern. Seed-bootstrap
intervals are likewise descriptive variability summaries over the retained seed ensemble, not independent
scenario-population confidence intervals. Inference is CI-based at the predeclared 95% level (the two-sided
upper-bound rule, stricter than a one-sided α = 0.05 Wilcoxon); p-values are shown only for comparability,
and the CI-based decisions carry the same familywise-multiplicity caveat as the p-values. Every p-value is
a descriptive diagnostic, not a confirmatory test. No familywise multiple-comparison correction is claimed
for any individual table; for completeness S-Table 39 reports Holm familywise- and Benjamini–Hochberg
false-discovery-rate–adjusted values over the entire family of displayed pairwise Wilcoxon p-values [23, 4],
and the qualitative ordering and the disciplined negative are unchanged under both adjustments.

[−7.0, +38.8] m). On the higher-fidelity slice (S-Table 25) the predeclared positive criterion likewise fails

the predeclared PUKF evidence. PUKF is therefore a predeclared bounded negative at both fidelities, with
an added post-hoc comparability stress, and the remaining diagnostic focus is structural (Sections 5.6, 5.7)
rather than statistical noise adaptation alone.

5.6

The compact DMC-EKF structural-channel check does not beat UKF on the higher-fidelity force-mismatch

5.7

Predeclared parametric structural-channel response (DSA-EKF)

DSA-EKF gives a statistically detectable but below-floor improvement on the 40-minute higher-fidelity slice,
so it remains a predeclared negative. One diagnostic concern for the Cartesian channel of Section 5.6 is that
a generic per-axis empirical acceleration may be the less specific structural channel for the actual mismatch
(drag/density/ballistic-coefficient bias); the standard LEO structural recipe estimates the multiplicative
drag-scale parameter directly [72, 81, 42]; learned per-satellite drag-coefficient estimation has also been
pursued for OD and prediction [8], whereas the diagnostic here shows a parametric drag-scale channel is inert
under sparse visibility. S-Table 27 reports the predeclared seven-state DSA-EKF on the same higher-fidelity
slice as DMC-EKF: the augmented state is [r, v, β] with β a first-order Gauss–Markov drag-scale parameter
about its nominal value of one, and the deterministic flow scales the drag acceleration by β(t) on top of
the otherwise compact two-body+J2 +drag model. The Gauss–Markov hyperparameters were selected on a
disjoint validation seed over a four-point predeclared grid before the held-out test; the selection record and
timestamped rule are retained for inspection. The predeclared positive criterion (DSA-EKF strictly lowest in
mean, strictly negative paired CI versus the best non-DSA comparator, gap above the 3% practical significance
floor defined in the statistical protocol) is not satisfied: DSA-EKF gives the lowest mean (347.67 m versus
EKF 354.69 m), and the DSA-EKF−EKF paired-bootstrap CI is strictly below zero (−7.0 m, [−9.7, −4.8] m),
but the absolute gap lies below the predeclared 10.6 m floor. The associated Wilcoxon signed-rank descriptive
p-value on the same pairing is highly significant (p ≈ 1.8 × 10−9 ); we report this as an explicit illustration of
the manuscript’s predeclared posture that statistical detectability is not the same as practical significance —
a strictly negative paired CI versus the EKF coexists here with a reported, statistically robust within-floor
improvement of about 7 m, and the predeclared rule therefore returns a bounded negative regardless of the
underlying p-value. A below-floor strictly-negative CI of this kind does not imply that the structural channel
provides zero benefit; below-floor is not the same as inert, but it is also not a positive contribution under
the predeclared rule because the measured benefit is smaller than the practical significance threshold. The
estimated drag-scale parameter moves by only |β̂ − 1| ∼ 10−7 from its prior over the 40-minute filter window,
confirming that the parametric channel is essentially unobservable from sparse line-of-sight measurements at
this window length: insufficient drag-induced orbit decay accumulates in 40 min to constrain β. We report
this as a predeclared parametric-structural-channel bounded negative alongside the Cartesian DMC bounded
negative, so the audit closes both standard structural responses to dynamics bias under sparse visibility, not
only one.

to the true multiplicative drag-scale bias and identifies EKF analytic linearisation as the binding failure mode
for the EKF-based candidate. The second slice substitutes deterministic sigma-point propagation of the
augmented flow, removes that failure mode, and leaves a separate sparse-visibility observability-side bound:
DSA-UKF is near UKF/AUKF in RMSE but β̂ remains effectively at one. The third slice enriches the
geometry and extends the arc; even with 9.6 km median arc-mean drag-induced separation, median trajectorymean β̂ = 1.0000 and median maximum |β̂ − 1| = 0.0010, isolating candidate-side β prior inertia while

paper’s central non-self-referential diagnostic: it localises the structural-channel failure to a specific binding
constraint rather than re-measuring a known mode. The inertia is consistent with an identifiability argument:
the line-of-sight innovation is absorbed by the well-observed position/velocity correction while β enters only
through twice-integrated drag, so the minimum-variance update attributes a position offset to r, v rather
than to β, and the favorable-geometry sanity check (Table 5) recovers β precisely because that aliasing is
suppressed. The full grid and stability caveats are in supplementary Section 8.

5.11

DBAR (predeclared heuristic, withdrawn)

DBAR is withdrawn because the powered simulator characterization does not exceed its no-information
baseline and the compact public SP3 replay does not transfer. The mechanism diagnostic above motivated
a predeclared decision rule (the predeclared online statistic for the Dynamics-Bias Adaptation-Risk, or
DBAR, indicator). Under a predeclared 450-realization characterization designed to test improvement over
an 81.3% no-information majority baseline, DBAR is not statistically distinguishable from that baseline
(Wilson 95% interval contains it). The original ten-arc public precise-reference probe on real LAGEOS
ranges against an independent ILRS SP3 product is retained as an archival diagnostic slice (6/10 correct,
0.60 accuracy, below the 0.80 always-no-fire majority baseline). The formal 210-arc compact recursive-filter
replay supersedes the extended 80-arc replay as the strongest compact real-data diagnostic and strengthens
the negative transfer readout. It attempted 210 arcs and completed 200; the 10 excluded arcs are explicit
insufficient-observation records, not hidden or imputed. DBAR is correct on 137/200 arcs (0.685), below the
0.760 no-fire majority baseline, with sensitivity 0.4375 and specificity 0.7632; pooled held-out RMSE is EKF
605.68 m, fixed-noise UKF 597.68 m, AUKF 630.65 m, and SP3-IC propagation 866.86 m. Paired differences
use first-minus-second, so negative means the second method has larger held-out RMSE; EKF-minus-AUKF
has mean −24.97 m, median 0.30 m, and 95% CI [−89.22, 26.34] m, while fixed-noise-UKF-minus-AUKF
has mean −32.97 m, median 0.00 m, and CI [−93.41, 13.05] m. The 200 completed arcs clear the earlier
approximate 185 comparable-arc formal power target, but the mean direction is not in AUKF’s favor and
both intervals cross zero, so this is not a positive AUKF result. SP3-IC propagation is best on 78/200 arcs
despite its worse pooled mean, concentrated in low-information arcs; it is treated as a propagation floor
and stress indicator, not estimator skill (per-stratum detail in the supplement). The powered simulator
characterization and failed public transfer are therefore the withdrawal evidence. We withdraw DBAR as a
claimed positive and report it as a bounded negative under the predeclared decision rule. Table 8 gives the
powered characterization in the main text, while row-level exhibition details remain in the supplement; the
underlying covariance-matching failure mode is known prior art [44, 60, 27, 70].

estimator skill and do not validate the simulator result. The public precise-reference layer is a bounded

Recent ILRS-supervised TLE/state-vector refinement and hybrid SGP4 error-learning studies reinforce why

5.15

Less-constrained learned residual and residual-scale sweep

Relaxing the residual bound does not create a learned positive; the less-constrained and larger-scale residual
checks fail across the tested scenarios. A possible concern is that the central negative could be preordained
by the bound : the canonical residual is hard-bounded (0.03 scaled-unit tanh) and additionally damped by a
learned gate, context budget, and prior-anchoring penalty, so any competitiveness with the classical prior
bank may be inherited “by construction”. We test this with a less-constrained learned residual comparator
(RGR-U, S-Table 59): identical architecture/capacity/inputs/curriculum, but the residual budget, gate,
context budget, and prior-anchoring/activity/entropy/visibility training penalties all removed. Removing
the bound does not produce a learned advantage: the paired RGR-U-minus-best-classical observed-step
gap exceeds seven kilometres on every scenario. This divergence is consistent with sparse-visibility training
instability for unbounded residuals and should not be read as a clean single-factor ablation of only the residual
bound. To address whether the canonical-vs-unconstrained gap is binary or graded, we additionally trained
two intermediate residual-scale comparators (tethered s = 0.30 and tethered s = 1.00); the residual-scale
sweep (S-Table 60) still shows graded collapse in this fixed architecture and budget, with the gap growing
monotonically with the residual scale on the tethered branch and becoming an order of magnitude larger
on the untethered endpoint. Taken together, RGR-U and the residual-scale sweep establish only a graded,
construction- and budget-conditioned collapse rather than a purely binary failure; they do not establish a
clean single-factor bound ablation or architecture-independent monotonicity.
This compact-simulator negative is informative but not externally decisive. It would be self-fulfilling
if the audit only tested a tightly bounded residual against its own prior in sparse geometry. Instead, the
record also shows that roughly four-fifths of evaluated steps are zero-visible, that an offline WLS reference
can use the visible arc much more effectively, that RGR-U and residual-scale checks fail when residual bounds
and anchoring are relaxed, and that favorable all-visible structural-channel controls can recover matched
signals. The drag-scale cascade then adds a separate structural readout: even with a 9.6 km median arc-mean
drag-induced separation, the geometry-enriched DSA-UKF slice leaves the drag-scale estimate near its prior,
while the DSA-EKF/DSA-UKF cascade separates EKF linearisation, observability, and candidate-inertia
limits. These findings do not establish that learned OD corrections fail under richer physics or different
sensors, but they do show that the tested sparse-geometry/prior-inertia construction fails after less-bounded
residual and structural-channel escape routes were audited.

Limitations and Missing Experiments

Results are evaluation evidence for the documented simulation protocol, windows, baselines, and reported
tables. They do not validate the estimator for flight operations, real-time integrated testbed deployment,
safety-critical autonomy, or all sparse-visibility regimes. Only a single canonical untuned stochastic EnKF
boundary check is reported (S-Table 4); it does not displace the classical baselines on any slice, but tuned,
inflated, or localized EnKF variants and sequential Monte Carlo OD methods remain outside this comparator
set, so the bounded ensemble-Kalman negative should not be read as addressing the broader ensemble-filter
design space. Likewise, cislunar particle/Gaussian-mixture filters and coupled orbit-attitude/sensor-tasking
frameworks are cited as adjacent operational/filtering context, not as comparator baselines addressed by
the tested claims. Graph message passing remains a controlled diagnostic rather than an isolated causal
contribution—the visible-station mean-deviation aggregator reduces to the identity whenever a single station
is visible, so cross-station message passing is a structural no-op at exactly the zero- and single-visible-station
steps that dominate the evaluation window, which is why the capacity-matched controls cross zero rather than
indicating that relational structure is unhelpful in principle—and the auxiliary replay, controlled-synthetic,
and bounded public precise-reference slices are stress/provenance probes rather than operational OD.
The self-audit demonstration is a self-evaluation: the record is designed by the authors and applied
primarily to the authors’ own residual constructions, plus in-house learned-gain and adapted-transposition
probes. That structure is useful for falsifying the authors’ own claims before they are carried into stronger
claims, but it is not independent third-party validation of a reusable framework and it does not establish that
another group would instantiate the same seven ingredients or reach the same boundaries. The ingredient
definitions used in the novelty audit are likewise self-authored operational definitions; they make this audit
inspectable and falsifiable per cell, but they are not an externally ratified evaluation taxonomy. This negative
is architecture-family specific to the tested constructions and the compact sparse-visibility setting; independent
application by a separate research group is required before the template structure can be claimed as a validated
reusable framework, and the cited-literature audit is bounded to this self-authored operational record rather
than an externally ratified taxonomy.
The post-hoc endpoint-timeline concern qualifies the primary endpoint ranking evidence; it does not
drive the AUKF R-inflation or drag-scale cascade mechanism findings, and the DBAR withdrawal follows
its own characterization rather than learned endpoint selection. The designated next step for this specific
limitation is an externally timestamped digest of a prospective decision rule, committed before and bound
to an independent public precise-reference replication carried out by a separate party, with no rule content
disclosed at timestamping; this is future work, neither completed here nor claimed as external trial-registry
preregistration.
The public LAGEOS/SP3 compact-filter readout is only a bounded real-measurement adaptive-filter
transfer diagnostic for the DBAR/AUKF R-inflation warning: the statistic is computed from the real
AUKF-versus-fixed-UKF innovation and R-adaptation stream, while the outcome label is defined externally
from held-out SP3 state error, not from the DBAR statistic and not from a simulator label (S-Tables 45
and 46). The original ten-arc slice and extended 80-arc replay remain archival evidence; the formal 210-arc

The simulator omits several failure modes that bound interpretation of the negative results. It does not
include maneuvers, thrust events, attitude-coupled force errors, clock-estimation states, sensor timing biases,
heterogeneous station instrumentation, decoded RF observables, onboard GNSS ambiguity handling, or a
multi-class operational tracking network. Its estimator-side dynamics deliberately stay below an operational
force-model ceiling; the long-arc and public probes test selected pressure points, not the full space of highfidelity force, sensor, and reference-model errors. Consequently, a negative in this paper weighs against
advancing the tested claims within the documented sparse-visibility setting, but it does not transfer as a
negative about a different sensor geometry, mission class, or operational POD system.
The compact-physics ceiling is also a mechanism limitation, not only a venue-scope caveat. This is a
two-gate condition for a learned above-floor positive: a learned correction can only become a meaningful
above-floor improvement if the residual it sees is jointly observable from the configured visible measurements
and representable by the candidate relative to the prior/filter bank. Each evaluated negative fails at least one
gate: on the noise-side stress split the corrective signal is observable but already represented by the adaptive
AUKF prior, while on the force-side mismatch and the drag-scale cascade it is unobservable from sparse
line-of-sight geometry and/or unrepresentable by the compact prior. The condition is descriptive of the tested
constructions, not a general impossibility result. In this study the offline WLS, multi-revolution public-catalog,
and public precise-reference probes all show that the compact dynamics and sparse geometry dominate
large parts of the residual budget. The correct interpretation is therefore a pre-campaign evidence-triage
negative for the tested constructions under this compact prior, not a claim that learned correction could
never help once a different force model, sensor, or public-reference regime makes the residual observable and
representable.
The missing experiments are concrete: centimetre-level precise SLR reduction; real high-fidelity forceerror campaigns; maneuvers; powered, independently sized reference-state scoring of learned estimators on
real measurements beyond the bounded public probes; decoded RF measurements; independent external
confirmation or operational validation of the endpoint result; and faithful reproduction of specific published
learned-OD systems in their own documented settings. The five highest-priority follow-ups are topology
generalization to held-out satellites, stations, and geometries; high-fidelity force/sensor mismatch beyond the
current controlled split; selector generalization on held-out public campaigns; broader non-graph parametermatched ablations with public precise-reference validation; and independent public precise-reference validation
beyond replay-style evidence.
Public replay scope and deployment boundary. The robustness claims are limited to the synthetic
measurement, visibility, and noise regimes summarized in the reported tables and figures. Under the timealigned SatNOGS observation-window replay (Appendix 15) the EKF stays finite at kilometre scale and offline
robust batch WLS produces an accurate postfit OD solution, so that slice is a bounded classical/offline-OD
validation, not a universal-failure slice or confirmatory deployment evidence [39].

Conclusion

The primary finding is a compact-simulator AUKF R-inflation diagnostic: under force-model mismatch in a
compact two-body+J2 +drag OD simulator, AUKF inflates effective R (mean 3.32; median R-only NIS 4.73
versus 1.60/1.83 for EKF/UKF) and damps needed state corrections, quantifying a compact-simulator instance
of the known covariance-matching failure mode. The drag-scale cascade separates EKF linearisation, sparsegeometry observability, and candidate-inertia limits as distinct binding constraints. The mechanism result is
deterministic and reproducible within the tested compact-model force-mismatch regime. Compact-model
covariance matching can damp needed corrections by treating dynamics-bias innovations as measurement-noise
evidence, a known limitation whose OD-specific form is further supported by the R-inflation cap control
(S-Table 28), while the supplement retains a long-arc EKF/AUKF stress observation that confines the
compact-J2 prescription to its tested regime and motivates confirmation before compact-model prescriptions
are transferred.
The bounded negative on learned estimators is explicitly secondary: no evaluated learned construction
beats the per-scenario best tuned classical reference on the primary observed-step endpoint, including the
stress-only K = 96 floor-power-scale check for approximately 0.80 power at the 27.5 m stress-floor effect.
The endpoint-choice sensitivity audit finds no learned positive under either the observed-step or all-step
endpoint. The structural-channel and DBAR diagnostics likewise fail their predeclared positive criteria. The
observed-step endpoint was selected post hoc on training data; K = 32 and K = 96 draws are internal
frozen-rule consistency checks, not external preregistration (Section 4.5). This learned-estimator bounded
negative is exploratory evidence under post-hoc endpoint selection and would require prospective external
confirmation for confirmatory status. The learned negative is architecture-family specific to the bounded
tanh-residual, prior-anchored RGR-GF family and tested comparators; RGR-U and residual-scale checks
limit, but do not eliminate, the concern that bound removal or prior anchoring contributes to the result.

The transferable element is the audit discipline, not the empirical result. Future OD studies would need
to instantiate their own endpoint hierarchy, frozen decision rules, classical and offline guardrails, capacitymatched controls, practical floors, and evidence records before claiming transfer. For small-satellite teams, the
immediate operational use is a campaign-planning filter-selection gate: require each candidate to survive tuned
adaptive guardrails, frozen endpoints, practical floors, and provenance checks on the compact-simulator proxy
before committing to a resource-intensive precise-reference validation run. Future confirmatory use would
need a prospectively fixed decision rule and independent validation or operational-reference confirmation
before evaluation; no such external confirmation is completed here. No operational POD, broad learned-OD
refutation, or independently validated protocol is claimed.
